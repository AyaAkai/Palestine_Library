How did the conflict start?

Britain took control of Palestine after defeating the Ottoman Empire, which ruled this part of the Middle East, in World War I. This land was inhabited by an Arab majority and a Jewish minority.

The Gaza War: Will the Arab Street’s position change its course?

Tensions grew between the two sides when the international community gave Britain the task of establishing a “national homeland” for the Jewish people in Palestine, which for the Jews represents the land of their ancestors, and the same is also the case for the Palestinian Arabs who see it as their land, so they opposed this step. In the period between the 1920s and the 1940s, the number of Jews coming to Palestine increased, many of whom were fleeing the religious persecution they were subjected to in Europe, searching for a homeland in the wake of what was known as the Holocaust in World War II. Violence also grew between Jews and Arabs or against British rule in the region. In 1947, the United Nations voted on a resolution to divide Palestine into two separate states, one Jewish and the other Arab, with Jerusalem becoming an international city. The Jewish leaders agreed to this plan, which was rejected by the Arab side. It was never implemented.

What is the Gaza Strip?

The Gaza Strip is an area in the form of a narrow strip, 41 kilometers (25 miles) long and 10 kilometers wide. It is located between Israel, Egypt, and the Mediterranean Sea. It is inhabited by about 2.3 million Palestinians, and is considered one of the regions with the highest population density in the world. Following the 1948-49 war, Egypt occupied Gaza for 19 years. In the 1967 war, Israel occupied the Gaza Strip and remained there until 2005, during which time it built Jewish settlements. Although Israel withdrew its forces and settlers from Gaza in 2005, it still controls its airspace, common borders, and beaches. The United Nations still considers the area occupied by Israel.
